I. Introduction
The basketball court in Barangay Sto. Niño has long served as a place where residents gather,
build friendships, and strengthen community ties. For many years, it has been a central venue for
recreation and social interaction. However, as personal schedules have become busier due to
work, school, and other responsibilities, the use of the court has become less organized and
people often go only when it is convenient.
Because of this, conflicts in scheduling and misunderstandings sometimes occur when multiple
groups want to use the court at the same time. To address this concern, we propose the
development of a Digital Basketball Court Scheduling System that will help organize court
reservations and monitor availability.

II. Objectives
The proposed system aims to:
1. Provide an organized reservation system for the barangay basketball court.
2. The objective is to prevent scheduling overlaps, maximize facility utilization, and provide
a clear, organized overview of reserved and available court time slots.
3. Allow barangay personnel to record and manage court reservations efficiently.
4. Maintain a systematic and updated record of basketball court schedules within the
system.
III. Proposed System Description
The Digital Basketball Court Scheduling System will be a computer based offline system
designed specifically for Barangay Sto. Niño. The system will be installed on a computer located
in the barangay office where reservations can be managed and monitored.
The basketball court in Barangay Sto. Niño is free for residents to use, therefore the barangay
prefers to manage reservations directly to ensure that the court is used properly and fairly by the
community. Because of this, the system will operate offline and will only be accessible through
the barangay office.
Residents or court users may visit the barangay office to check available time slots and request
reservations. Authorized barangay personnel will encode reservation details into the system and
update the court schedule.

Barangay officials or authorized personnel will have administrative access to:
• Monitor available time slots for the basketball court
• Record and manage reservations for specific dates and times
• Monitor scheduled activities and reservations
•This system will provide a simple and organized way for the barangay to manage court
reservations while keeping the use of the facility free and accessible to barangay officials.
IV. Expected Benefits
The implementation of this system will:
• Reduce conflicts and double bookings of the basketball court
• Efficient time for residents and barangay personnel when checking schedules
• Ensure fair access to the basketball court for all residents
• Support organized the management of the scheduling system
V. Target Beneficiaries
• Residents of Barangay Sto. Niño
• Youth organizations and sports groups
• Barangay officials and administrators

VI. Scope of the Study
The Digital Basketball Court Scheduling System will be developed specifically for the basketball
court located in Barangay Sto. Niño. The system will operate offline and will be installed on a
computer in the barangay office.
The system will include the following functions:
• Monitor available basketball court schedules
• Reservation requests are submitted by residents and approved by the Barangay.
• Displaying reserved and available time slots
• Managing reservation records through an administrative interface
• If no slot is available today, the system suggests the nearest available time; if the resident
misses it, the request is marked as “Missed.”
VII. Limitations of the Study
• Although the system can be deployed online, the barangay officials prefer an offline setup, so it
will only be accessible on the computer where it is installed.
• Residents cannot reserve the court remotely and must coordinate with barangay personnel.
• The scheduling of the basketball court will follow the policies set by Barangay Sto. Niño.
