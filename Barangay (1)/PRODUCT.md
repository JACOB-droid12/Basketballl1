# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Barangay Sto. Nino personnel use this system on an office computer to receive resident requests, check court availability, encode reservation details, approve or decline bookings, and keep a clear record of court use. Secondary beneficiaries are residents, youth groups, sports groups, and barangay officials who need fair access to the basketball court without remote self-service.

The working context is practical and in-person: a resident visits the barangay office, staff checks the schedule, and the staff member records or updates the reservation while keeping the queue moving.

## Product Purpose

This product is an offline basketball court scheduling system for Barangay Sto. Nino. It exists to prevent overlapping reservations, make available and reserved time slots visible, maintain an updated reservation record, and help staff mark pending, approved, completed, missed, and conflict-prone bookings.

Success means barangay personnel can answer "is the court available?", record a request, explain conflicts or nearest available slots, and produce a simple summary without needing internet access or technical support.

## Brand Personality

Clear, civic, patient.

The interface should feel like a reliable barangay office tool: plain-spoken, official enough to trust, and friendly enough for non-technical staff. It should prioritize readable labels, visible actions, and bilingual support over decorative polish.

## Anti-references

Avoid a public startup landing page, a sports entertainment app, a dark command-center dashboard, tiny admin-table typography, jargon-heavy workflow labels, decorative glassmorphism, neon gradients, and hidden controls that require training to discover.

Avoid treating residents as direct online users. The current scope is staff-mediated, offline, and office-based.

## Design Principles

1. One office task at a time. Each screen should make the next staff action obvious: check, encode, approve, print, or review.
2. Plain labels beat clever labels. Use direct English with Filipino support where it lowers staff hesitation.
3. Make schedule status visible before details. Pending, approved, completed, missed, and conflict states should be distinguishable at a glance.
4. Design for shared office computers. Favor large hit targets, durable contrast, persistent navigation, and forgiving forms.
5. Offline confidence matters. The product should feel complete and trustworthy without cloud language, remote booking assumptions, or internet-dependent flows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast as a baseline. Body text should stay comfortably readable for office use, with the staff-friendly variant using a 17px base size and 48px or taller primary controls. Status colors must be reinforced with labels, not color alone. Motion should be short and functional, limited to feedback for hover, focus, dialogs, and state changes.
